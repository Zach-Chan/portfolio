VCH Learning Technologies
Concussion

In a 4 month long project, I co-designed and developed a web tool that helps guide concussion patients and their loved ones with learning about MTBI (Mild Traumatic Brain Injury) and how to manage the recovery process.

Process

The Client's Vision

I was brought on board the project because of my multidisciplinary background as a designer and web developer. In such a small team, this was a huge advantage that helped us adapt to changes in client needs and pivots in direction. As a team, we met with our client: the clinical therapists at GF Strong Hospital in Vancouver BC. We discussed and sketched and created lots of ideas together to refine the direction of the project and get a stronger idea of what the problem area was and what avenues we had available for pursuing solutions.

GF Strong provides workshops and personalized therapy for people diagnosed with MTBI and strokes. Many people experience MTBI in the form of concussions from recreational activities, car accidents, and work. However, GF Strong’s workshops for concussion are always booked months in advance which puts people who are in need of information and guidance on how to recover waiting unacceptable lengths of time. GF Strong’s services have a huge impact on helping patients get onto the road to recovery and being unable to offer enough services is the number one obstacle they want to overcome.

Defining Scope

Making the app comfortable to use despite language barriers was a major area of focus for me. I ideated through sketches to design an onboarding process to select a user's native language and providing clear interface elements to quickly swap between languages to use the app while also learning useful English words.

Desgin Pillars

We designed initial iterations of the app interface with Adobe Illustrator but completed designing in the UX program Flinto so that we could capture the animated responses we had designed and conduct usability testing with an interactive prototype. I conducted informal speak aloud tests to gain qualitative feedback and find problem areas of the UI.

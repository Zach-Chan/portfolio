Run Tracking Kiosk

I conducted user research with methods like cultural probing and conducting interviews to define pain points in our user group. I also animated UI elements and a final concept video to present the digital solution I designed with two team members

Context

Fit First is a running shoe store that provides expert knowledge to their customers to help them improve their running ability by finding the best shoe for them and coaching them in their weekly Sunday running clinic and club.

Problem

The Fit First running club has running members of all different skill levels who express different levels of commitment and personal goals that encourage them to exercise with the group.

Process

Understanding Users

Fit First’s members consists people ranging from young adults who hadn’t run for years to retirees who are marathon veterans. To gain a better understanding of the people in the group and identify an opportunity for a digital solution I conducted interviews with members and observed and participated in group activities to gather information. We found lots of overlap in motivations and pain points along experience-level divisions and the organizers themselves so we created 3 personas from our research to guide our decision-making process throughout the rest of the project: the Beginner Runner, the Intermediate Runner, and the Run Organizer.

Identifying an Opportunity

All three of these users’ goals centered around how to motivate themselves (or their members in the case of the organizer) to run more often to keep improving their fitness. The different types of users had pain points like lack of time, self-consciousness about their fitness, and physical pain which kept them from reaching their goals. However they all still beat these hurdles each week because they loved their weekly Sunday runs with the group. We realized our solution would need to capture elements of what they love about the social group runs to motivate members to do additional running outside of that.

Testing Theories

To see if we could encourage members to run more often each week I ran an activity to test if a collaborative running distance goal would affect the amount that members ran on on their own. I designed the goal as a tangible item to see if a physical representation of the goal would help motivate them more than having normal assigned homework runs. I put a paper with a table onto a relay baton for runners to fill in the distances they ran and then physically pass the baton onto their friends in the group to keep adding to the goal. On post-activity interviews, three more members who normally don’t complete homework runs achieved them and attributed the baton to helping keep them more motivated. This guided us to our final solution of creating a digital interface to help members continue to interact with each other and keep each other accountable while striving towards a collective goal together.

Animating UI Elements

When designing the interface of the community running distance tracker I animated the total distance ran hero graphic and the day/month/year graph data representation elements of the UI. I wanted to convey a sense of progress to users and designed the elements to spring forward and fill out the data element to feel like a map marker plotting a route and give a sense of the distance covered towards their goals.

Filming and Editing

After our concept prototype was complete I filmed and edited a showcase video to once again frame the problem we uncovered, the digital product we designed, and what the effects of our solution would be for the running group. I used a Nikon D700 for filming, Adobe AfterEffects to create the motion graphics, and Adobe Premier to edit footage, effects, and sound.

Reflection

During this project I learned and used new research methods like conducting cultural probes and creating informance videos. From gathering and analyzing the data gathered I gained a lot of experience in pinpointing key needs to design for that are in line with users' values and goals. I also became adept at representing my findings to share with others in weekly critiques through different mediums like print and video. I know being able to articulate and share my findings and design process in so many forms will be a great asset in future collaborative projects.
